The Technical Score is a weighted composite of six sub-scores that together grade a stock's technical setup. Each sub-score ranges from 0.0 to 1.0 and the final composite is clamped to [0.0, 1.0].

The six components and their weights are: trend (25%), momentum (20%), RSI (20%), MACD (15%), volume (10%), and ATR (10%). Trend measures distance from the 50-day EMA — above +5% scores 1.0, above the EMA scores 0.6, within −5% scores 0.3, below scores 0.0. Momentum looks at the 63-day return. RSI scores highest in the 45–70 band.

The composite is then used in the M8 ranking system, where it is combined with insider and momentum signals to produce a final ranking score. A Technical Score above 0.5 is required to pass the initial gate.

**Key takeaway:** Six technical factors are weighted into a single 0–1 score. Trend and momentum carry the most weight.
