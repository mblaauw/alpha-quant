title: "Fundamental Quality Gate"
difficulty: intermediate
---

The M4 Fundamental Quality gate screens stocks on four fundamental criteria. It is a pass/fail gate — if any criterion fails, the stock is excluded from further consideration.

The four checks are: positive operating cash flow (OCF must be > 0), manageable debt-to-equity ratio (D/E must be less than 2× the sector median), reasonable accrual ratio (between −0.05 and 0.05), and no negative earnings surprise (if earnings data is available, actual EPS must not be more than 10% below estimates).

If all fundamental data is missing, the gate passes in degraded mode — it logs the reason but does not block the trade. This handles stocks without recent filings. The gate is designed to catch the most common red flags: companies burning cash, dangerously leveraged, or inflating earnings through accounting adjustments.

**Key takeaway:** Four fundamental screens catch the worst offenders. Missing data passes in degraded mode.
