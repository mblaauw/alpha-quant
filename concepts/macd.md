The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two exponential moving averages of a security's price. It consists of the MACD line (12-period EMA minus 26-period EMA), the signal line (9-period EMA of the MACD line), and the histogram (MACD line minus signal line).

A positive MACD histogram means the short-term EMA is pulling away from the long-term EMA — upward momentum is accelerating. A negative histogram means momentum is slowing. In Alpha Quant, a positive MACD histogram contributes positively to the technical score, while a negative one scores zero.

MACD works best in trending markets. In range-bound markets it can generate false signals, which is why it is only one of several indicators weighted in the composite score.

**Key takeaway:** A rising MACD histogram confirms upward momentum. It contributes to the technical score when positive.
